Understanding Water Types in Sugarcane Cultivation

Water footprint in sugarcane agriculture is a complex ecosystem of resource management. Our tool helps you understand and optimize water usage by breaking down different water components.

Green Water

Rainwater stored in soil, directly used by sugarcane crops. Essential for natural growth and representing nature's irrigation.

Blue Water

Water from irrigation systems, surface water, and groundwater sources. Managed water supply crucial for sugarcane production.

Grey Water

Water required to neutralize agricultural pollutants from fertilizers and chemicals. Indicates environmental neutralization and sustainability.
